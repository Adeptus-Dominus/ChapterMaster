FORCES FROM POPULATIONS & THE BIGGER FIGHT - the short version
==============================================================
(design direction - not built yet)


THE CORE CHANGE
---------------
Right now every enemy on a world is an abstract "force level" 1-6, and
each level spawns a hand-written list of units. We're replacing that:
every race has a REAL POPULATION on each world, and the forces in a
battle are drawn straight from those numbers. A WAAAGH that's festered
for 40 turns fields far more than one that just landed.


HOW POPULATIONS WORK
--------------------
* Tracked per region, per race - so forces spread out and shift region
  by region.
* Mostly CIVILIAN. Only a fraction actually fights (the combatants); the
  civilians are the base that breeds them, grows over time, and gets
  consumed or taxed.
* Total-war races are the exception: Tyranids and Orks are basically all
  fighters (Gretchin are the closest thing Orks have to a civ class), so
  they field their whole population.


THE IMPERIAL SIDE  (civilians -> PDF -> tithe -> Guard)
-------------------------------------------------------
* Civilians raise a base PDF for local defence.
* The Imperial Tithe trains one in ten of the PDF - the best of them -
  into Astra Militarum (Guard) regiments that ship out to the wider war.
* We add a NEW PDF unit: a weaker Guardsman, so locals can actually put
  up a fight against invaders.


FLEETS DROP POPULATIONS
-----------------------
* Instead of a fleet "dumping force", an arriving fleet drops a
  POPULATION - a beachhead / spore-drop seeded into a region.
* From there populations interact directly: they grow, spread, and
  CONSUME each other and the locals. Orks bloom and use captives as
  livestock; Nids eat everything into biomass.
* Faction-vs-faction fights become population vs population - each side
  musters what it actually has, and the loser's population takes the
  casualties.


THE FIGHT ITSELF
----------------
* Combat is a NUMBERS-VS-NUMBERS simulation - stacks of troops trading
  firepower, casualties = damage / toughness. It already handles huge
  counts, so a million-strong battle resolves fine.
* Marines stay real units with elite stats. They add their devastation
  and TIP THE BALANCE of a fight - but they are NOT an auto-win. A
  thousand Astartes can't out-body a million Orks on their own.
* So you fight ALONGSIDE allied PDF and Guard. The mass holds the line;
  the Marines are the hammer that breaks it.


THE SCALE  (straight from 40K lore)
-----------------------------------
* Hive worlds: hundreds of billions to over a trillion people.
* PDF: about 0.5% of the population - millions to billions on a big world.
* Guard regiments: 10,000-80,000, raised as 1 in 10 of the PDF.
* Ork WAAAGHs: millions typically, billions at their worst.
  => Think MILLIONS, not the ~11,000 the current battles cap at.


STARTING STATES
---------------
* Orks start a few years into their growth cycle, just beginning to
  attack the locals.
* Tau start owning their worlds, clustered together, expanding
  methodically outward.


HOW IT GETS BUILT
-----------------
* One faction at a time, ORKS FIRST. The Orks already have every unit
  type in the game, so it's just drawing them from a population by ratio
  instead of a fixed tier. For example, a 1,000,000-strong WAAAGH would
  field roughly:
      280k Shoota Boyz, 250k Slugga Boyz, 200k 'Ard Boyz, 250k Gretchin,
      ~13k Tankbustas, ~7k Flash Gitz, ~6k Nobz, ~3k Kommandos,
      ~6k Deff Dreads, ~3k Battlewagons, and one Big Warboss.
* Then Tyranids (biomass), then Tau, then the rest.
