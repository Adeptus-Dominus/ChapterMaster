====================================================================
 PART A - WHAT'S BUILT
====================================================================

1. MULTI-REGION PLANETS (data layer)
   - Every planet = a Capital + a variable number of outlying regions
     (count by world type: Hive/Forge/Temperate/Shrine/Feudal/Desert
     = 4; Ice/Agri/Death = 3; Lava = 2; Dead/Daemon/Craftworld/empty
     = 1).
   - Each region has its own owner, population, pdf, guardsmen,
     fortification, defences, upgrades, buildings.
   - Stored in obj_star.p_regions[planet] as plain-data Region records
     (scripts/Region/Region.gml). Persists automatically via the
     existing p_* save path. Generated at worldgen, lazily for old
     saves.

2. CONQUEST OVERLAY (Option A - planet scalars stay authoritative)
   - Each turn regions_sync() DERIVES region control from the real
     state: the owner holds the Capital; a contesting force takes a
     share of the outlying regions scaled by strength. Grind an enemy
     down and its regions flip to you one at a time, capital last.
   - Nothing writes back to the authoritative scalars, so real combat
     losses are never clobbered.

3. REGION-SELECTION UI + STEERED CONQUEST
   - A "Planetary Regions" panel on the system view: each region's
     owner (colour-coded), fortification, defences, garrison, plus a
     CONTESTED badge.
   - Click a region to set your CONQUEST FOCUS. regions_sync flips the
     focused region first, otherwise weakest-fortification-first - so
     defences resist capture and you steer the conquest.
   - Left-clicking a multi-region planet now locks in the regions view
     (previously only hover showed it; click opened the garrison
     report). Fix in PlanetData.set_star_select_planet.
   - Region zone names are now RANDOMISED per planet from a ~32-name
     pool (no more identical "Northern Reaches" every world).

4. PER-REGION DEFENCES + CONSUME ON CAPTURE
   - Fortified regions resist flipping and are ground down as they're
     taken (consume-on-capture) - all inside the overlay, so the
     fragile combat core is untouched. (Making the tactical battle
     screen itself region-aware is deferred Option B.)

5. REGION BUILDING TREE (construction)
   - Data-driven catalogue (region_building_catalogue). A Construction
     box under the regions panel with compact holo build tiles
     (reuses the Monastery->Forge holo widget pattern). Instant
     requisition build; buildings stored in Region.buildings.
   - BUILDINGS (per region unless noted):
       Bastion         1500  max 5   any     +1 fortification (cap 5)
       Turret Battery  1000  max 5   any     +1 defences (cap 5)
       Anti-Orbital    8000  1/PLANET any     active weapon (see below)
       Manufactorum   10000  max 1   Hive/Forge/Desert  +~100 forge pts/turn
       Factory         2000  max 1   Hive/Forge/Temperate +4 req/turn
       Mine            1500  max 1   Desert/Ice/Lava/Dead/Death +3 req/turn
       Industrial Farms 1200 max 1   Agri/Temperate/Feudal  grows population
       PDF Barracks    1000  max 1   any     +200 PDF/turn
       Guard Barracks  1500  max 1   any     +100 Guardsmen/turn
       Training Ground 1200  max 1   any     +XP to Scouts garrisoned here
       Candidate Stn.  2000  max 1   any     +15 recruit screening (no apoths)
   - CAPS: Bastion & Turret stack (5 each). Anti-Orbital = ONE PER
     PLANET. Each region may hold only ONE player-benefit improvement
     (Manufactorum OR Factory OR Mine ...), but defences build
     alongside it. Enforced by region_building_can_build; the panel
     only lists what's currently buildable.
   - Per-turn effects tick from scr_star_ownership's real (argument0)
     pass, only for regions you hold.

6. ANTI-ORBITAL GUN (region-owner-based, double-edged)
   - Fires for WHOEVER holds its region. Player/Imperial-held: destroys
     one hostile ship in orbit each turn. If the enemy captures that
     region, the gun turns on YOUR fleet - a double-edged sword.
   - Faction rules: mindless Tyranids can't work it (silent); a
     Genestealer Cult must first learn it (~50%/turn) and then keeps
     firing until driven off the region (tracked by Region.gun_mastered;
     a reformed cult relearns); all other factions use it normally.
   - Player-ship removal mirrors the game's own space-combat method
     (find a live ship id, zero obj_ini.ship_hp, drop the count).

====================================================================
 PART B - DESIGN PLAN (populations, biomass, force generation)
====================================================================
 Status: DOCUMENTED, not coded. Full spec in
 docs/POPULATIONS_FORCE_PLAN.md. Ork-first, phased.

THE UNIFIED VISION
   Replace the abstract 0-6 enemy "force level" with forces DRAWN FROM
   POPULATIONS. Every side - attacker or defender - musters exactly
   WHAT IT ACTUALLY HAS on the world: its per-region population, its
   p_pdf / p_guardsmen, its race / biomass pool. All of it flows
   through ONE shared generator (spawn_from_counts) driven by a
   per-faction LORE RECIPE. No abstract tiers, no nominal pools or
   muster rates - what is present is what takes the field. Orks are the
   pilot; PDF, Guard, Tau, Nids and the rest are the same machine with
   different recipes.

HOW COMBAT ACTUALLY WORKS (reviewed from the player's seat)
   - It's a NUMERICAL SIMULATION - counts trading firepower, "numbers
     beating numbers". Each side is vertical bars on a centre line; bar
     HEIGHT = strength, COUNT shows only on hover. No models, no map.
   - The player mostly presses ENTER to advance each round and reads
     the combat log ("Enemy Forces at X%"). One in-fight input: click a
     column to toggle advance/hold. All real choices are pre-battle:
     which forces to commit, target, ship bombardment, and a FORMATION
     (a saved 10-column layout).
   - The resolution MATH already scales to huge counts (damage/hp =
     kills). Enemies are stored as counts, so a 1,000,000-Ork battle
     resolves correctly.
   - What breaks at population scale (extra work): (a) the strength bar
     SATURATES at ~800 men - needs a rescale so millions read; (b)
     player-side MASS must be COUNTS, not per-model structs (marines
     are stored one-struct-per-model and rescanned every turn - fine
     for ~1000 Astartes, would hang for thousands of PDF). So: elites
     = real units, mass = counts.

NEW STATE (the D layer)
   - Absolute headcounts for EVERY faction (the Imperial-Guard
     headcount->units model, generalised).
   - Populations live PER REGION (Region.race_pop, indexed by faction);
     planet 0-6 level becomes a derived rollup. Tyranid biomass is a
     per-planet pool.
   - CIVILIANS vs COMBATANTS: a population is mostly civilian; only a
     combatant fraction takes the field (the Imperium already does
     this: population -> military -> PDF/Guard). Battle musters the
     combatants; the civ pool is the base that breeds them, grows, and
     gets consumed/taxed. TOTAL-WAR races are the exception - Tyranids
     (every bioform fights) and largely Orks (the whole mob fights;
     Gretchin are the nearest thing to a civ underclass) field ~all of
     it. Each race profile carries a levy rate or a total-war flag.

THE IMPERIAL PIPELINE (civ -> PDF -> tithe -> Guard)  [LORE-CONFIRMED]
   - Civilians raise a BASE PDF for local defence (lore ~0.5% of
     population).
   - The Imperial TITHE trains ONE-TENTH of the PDF - the best of them -
     into Astra Militarum (Guard) regiments that deploy off-world.
   - So: civ -> PDF (local, always) -> Guard ~= PDF/10 (tithed, better).
     This IS the Guard combat block's existing /10, now with a lore
     reason. A NEW PDF unit (a weaker Guardsman) is required so locals
     can actually fight.

LORE-GROUNDED SCALE  (think MILLIONS, not thousands)
   - Hive world: each hive 10-100 billion, 5-20 hives -> often >1
     trillion per world; doubles ~every 100 years.
   - PDF ~= 0.5% of population (a civilised world: ~20 million up into
     billions).
   - Guard regiment: 10,000-80,000 typical (siege 250,000+), raised as
     ~1/10 of PDF.
   - Ork WAAAGH: MILLIONS typically, BILLIONS at its worst (War of the
     Beast overran whole sectors).
   => The current combat caps at ~11,000 (threat 6); real scale is
      millions. This confirms the display rescale + counts-not-per-model
      work, and makes "marines tip the balance but can't win a WAAAGH
      alone" perfectly lore-accurate.

FLEETS DROP POPULATIONS (not force levels)
   - Instead of a fleet "dumping X force" (bumping the 0-6 level), a
     fleet arrival DROPS A POPULATION - a beachhead / spore-drop seeded
     into a region's race pool.
   - Populations then INTERACT directly: grow, spread, CONSUME each
     other and the locals (orks eat the populace as livestock; Nids eat
     everything -> biomass), and CONTEST regions (reusing the conquest
     overlay). NPC-vs-NPC becomes POOL vs POOL - each side musters what
     it has, the loser's pool takes real casualties - replacing the
     abstract choose(1..6)*score fight in scr_enemy_ai_a.

MARINES / ELITES
   - Real HEADCOUNT units, fighting as they do now with elite stats, in
     their actual small numbers. NOT abstracted into a multiplier. They
     add their DEVASTATION to the field and TIP THE BALANCE - but it's
     NOT an auto-win; a handful can't beat an overwhelming tide alone.

WHY FIGHT ALONGSIDE ALLIES
   - Worlds now field their own PDF/Guard, so defending a world means
     fighting alongside its defenders.
   - Marines can't out-body a million-Ork horde; the mass allied
     PDF/Guard IS the line, the Astartes are the hammer. The elite +
     the mass can win; the mass alone can't.
   - Lore-accurate combined arms, and groundwork for the
     Warmaster/Sector-Governor mode (lead Guard, petition the Astartes).
   - The game already has an ally path (scr_battle_allies, the
     ally/ally_forces setup in obj_ncombat) to build on.

FACTION BEHAVIOUR & STARTING POSTURES
   - Orks: start ~1-5 years into their growth cycle, "cracking open"
     the locals. Bloom + livestock + spore-spread + WAAAGH. Total war.
   - Tau: start OWNING their worlds, clustered together, expanding
     methodically outward (assimilate, don't exterminate).
   - Later: Tyranids (consume->biomass->ascend->splinter fleet),
     Chaos/Heretics (corrupt->traitor PDF/Guard), Necrons (awaken->cull),
     Eldar (raid).

ORK FORCE-COMPOSITION RECIPE  (the pilot; P = ork headcount)
   Calibrated to today's tiers (P~100 = old L1, P~11000 = old L6);
   every unit already exists, so it's "ratio-ise + validate", no new
   content. Mob = shares of P (normalised over active rows); retinue =
   1-per-N with thresholds; boss above thresholds.
     MOB:      Shoota 28% | Slugga 25% | 'Ard 20% (P>=150) | Gretchin 25% (P>=1500)
     RETINUE:  Nob/Meganob 1/130 boyz | Flash Git 1/150 (P>=800) |
               Mekboy 1/1250 | Deff Dread 1/180 | Battlewagon 1/400 |
               Tankbusta 1/75 (P>=5000) | Kommando 1/350 (P>=5000)
     BOSS:     Nob warlord (P<300) -> Warboss (P>=300) -> Big Warboss +
               2-3 waves (P>=9000)
   Example at P = 1,000,000: ~280k Shoota, 250k Slugga, 200k 'Ard, 250k
   Gretchin; ~13.3k Tankbustas, 6.7k Flash Gitz, 5.6k Nobz, 2.9k
   Kommandos, 800 Mekboyz; ~5.6k Deff Dreads, 2.5k Battlewagons; 1 Big
   Warboss. (An apocalyptic, sector-level event.)

IMPERIAL GUARD BLOCK (the working template, T = guard headcount)
   Guardsmen: T/10 (5 columns of T/50) | Ogryn max(10,T/6650) |
   Heavy Weapons Team T/10000 | Leman Russ Battle T/10000 |
   Leman Russ Demolisher T/20000 | Chimera T/20000 |
   Vendetta T/40000 | Basilisk T/50000.

BUILD ORDER (phased, one faction at a time; combat file last)
   1  Per-region race_pop + biomass state; seeds; Ork/Tau starting
      postures; a "pops" debug cheat.               (LOW risk)
   2  Bridge: 0-6 level derived from the region rollup; behaviour
      profiles (grow/spread/consume) retargeted to the pools; victory
      drains the pool.                               (MEDIUM)
   3  PDF unit + generalise the Guard headcount->units block into a
      shared spawn_from_counts; defender = PDF (p_pdf) + Guard
      (p_guardsmen).                                 (MEDIUM-HIGH)
   4  Unhook ORKS (attacker) via spawn_from_counts + the Ork recipe;
      threat carries the count; display rescale; mass-as-counts.
                                                      (HIGH - combat core)
   5+ Nids (biomass), then Tau/Chaos/Necrons/Eldar, one block at a time.
