SECTOR GOVERNOR EDITION - the short version
===========================================

WHAT'S IN NOW
-------------

* Planets are now REGIONS. Every world is a capital plus a few outlying
  regions, so you take a world region by region instead of in one click.
  Click a region on the system screen to focus your assault - the panel
  shows each region's owner, fortification, defences and garrison, and
  flags contested worlds (where more than one faction holds ground).

* BUILD on your regions. A construction panel lets you build per region:
    - Defences: Bastion (walls), Turret Battery, Anti-Orbital Gun.
    - Economy:  Manufactorum (forge/industry points), Factory, Mine.
    - Growth:   Industrial Farms, PDF Barracks, Guard Barracks,
                Training Ground (trains Scouts stationed on the world),
                Candidate Station (better recruiting without tying up
                your Apothecaries).
  Rules: one economy/growth "improvement" per region, but defences build
  alongside it. The Anti-Orbital Gun is one per planet.

* The ANTI-ORBITAL GUN is a double-edged sword. While you hold its region
  it shoots down an enemy ship in orbit every turn. If the enemy captures
  that region, the captured gun fires on YOUR fleet. Mindless Tyranids
  can't work it; a Genestealer Cult can figure it out - and keeps using
  it until you drive them off.

* Odds and ends: region zones now get randomised names, and clicking a
  planet properly brings up the regions view.


WHAT'S COMING  (designed, not built yet)
----------------------------------------

The big one: FORCES COME FROM POPULATIONS, not an abstract 1-6 number.

* Every race has a real population on each world, tracked per region.
  Battles are drawn from those actual numbers - a WAAAGH that's festered
  for 40 turns fields far more than one that just landed.

* Enemy fleets DROP A POPULATION (a beachhead), which then grows, spreads
  and eats the locals - Orks bloom and use captives as livestock, Nids
  consume everything into biomass.

* Populations are mostly CIVILIAN; only a fraction fights (except
  total-war races like Tyranids and Orks). Civilians raise the PDF; the
  Imperial tithe trains one in ten of the PDF into the Guard.

* A new PDF unit (a weaker Guardsman) so locals can actually fight off
  invasions.

* Real 40K scale: WAAAGHs in the millions, hive worlds in the trillions.
  Combat is a numbers-vs-numbers sim, so it handles the scale fine.

* You fight ALONGSIDE allied PDF/Guard. Your Marines tip the balance but
  can't beat a horde on their own - the mass holds the line, the Astartes
  are the hammer.

* Built one faction at a time, Orks first.


That's the brass tacks. Full detail lives in the design docs
(POPULATIONS_FORCE_PLAN.md, REGIONS.md, SECTOR_GOVERNOR_PLAN.md).
